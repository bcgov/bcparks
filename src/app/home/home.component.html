What We're Working On, and How

Over a decade ago, Tim O’Reilly, a well-known futurist, advisor to governments and founder of the terms open-source and Web 2.0, shared the idea of “Government as a Platform.” O’Reilly observed that, even then, people are “connected like never before, and have the skill sets and passion to solve problems affecting them.” Since then, much has been written about “GaaP” and the opportunities it provides to empower people to spark the innovations necessary to meet the challenges of the increasingly complex days we exist — and design and delivery public services — within.

British Columbia, long recognized as a leader in the application of new tools and methods to meeting public challenges, continues that trend today with its framework for being a digital-era government. Our intention at BC Parks is to be humble leaders in putting this framework into action, and through our work and this site convene a community across and beyond government to collaborate on fulfilling our shared mission for our parks and protected areas.

We hope you’ll join us, and have a look around here for your opportunities to do so.
